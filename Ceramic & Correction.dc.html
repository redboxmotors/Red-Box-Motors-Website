A mirror finish is made in the paint, then sealed.

Great gloss starts with the surface itself. We level swirls, holograms, wash marring and light scratches through multi-stage machine correction — bringing back the depth and clarity the factory finish is capable of.

Then we seal that corrected paint with a Carbon Collective ceramic coating: a hard, hydrophobic layer that beads water, resists staining and UV fade, and makes the whole car far easier to keep clean for years.

— Why a coating works
01
Hydrophobic

Water and road film sheet off the surface instead of clinging, so the car self-rinses and dries spot-free.

02
Chemical & UV

A hard ceramic layer shrugs off bird-etch, fallout, bug acids and the UV fade that dulls unprotected clear coat.

03
Slick & clean

Dirt struggles to bond to the low-friction surface — cutting wash times and swirl risk every time you clean it.

04
Depth & gloss

Over freshly corrected paint, the coating adds a wet, mirror-clarity shine that holds for years, not weeks.

— The process

Three stages to a lasting finish.

01
Decontaminate

Wash, clay and chemical decontamination strip bonded fallout and road grime so nothing gets ground into the paint before polishing.

02
Correct

Under controlled lighting we machine out swirls, holograms and marring across as many stages as the paint needs, then refine to full clarity.

03
Coat

The corrected surface is prepped and sealed with a Carbon Collective ceramic, then left to cure so the protection bonds hard.

— Coating + film

Coat the paint, or coat the film.

A ceramic can seal bare corrected paint or go straight over STEK paint protection film. On a car already wearing PPF, the coating adds slickness and stain resistance to the film's impact protection — the two work as one system, and we plan them together.

New delivery? We'll correct and coat before it ever sees a car wash, so the finish starts perfect and stays that way.
